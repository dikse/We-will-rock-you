Beats, bars and metres

A beat is the regular sound in music.

Beats go in groups. A group of beats is called bar.

On the musical lines, a bar is between two vertical lines.

Each bar has a number of beats.

Metre is how we count the beats in each bar. It tells us the rhythm.

- A duple metre has 2 beats in each bar.

- A triple metre has 3 beats in each bar.

Musical symbols and beats

In music, we have different notes.

Each note has:

– a name

– a symbol

– a rest (silence)

– a number of beats


Whole note and rest

It is the longest note.

It looks like a circle.

The rest is a rectangle under the line.

It has 4 beats.

Half note and rest

It looks like a circle with a stick.

The rest is a rectangle on top of the line.

It has 2 beats.

Quarter note and rest

It looks like a black circle with a stick.

The rest looks like a zigzag.

It has 1 beat.

Eighth note and rest

It has one flag.

The rest is a small curve.

It has 1/2 beats.

Sixteenth note and rest

It has two flags.

The rest has two small curves.

It has 1/4 beats.

3. Guess the Queen song!

You are doing great!

You know Queen, some of their songs, and also about musical notes and rests.

Now it’s time to use your ears, your hands, and your pencils!

Work in groups of 3.

Follow these steps:

1. Read the musicogram

A musicogram is a visual rhythm guide.

Click here to download the document “Play and guess the song”.

2. Play the rhythm with your pencils (or your hands)

Respect the duration of the notes and the rests when you play.

3. Talk together and guess the song!

It can be one of the three you studied in Queen’s anthems.
